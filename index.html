How it works

The Algaerithm pod

Sewage is discharged into UK rivers thousands of times a year, but that data is hard to access. So how do you know if this water is safe for you, your children, your dog?

Inside the floating pod in this river lives a colony of Chlamydomonas – Chlamy for short – a microscopic freshwater alga living in water sampled from this exact stretch of river.

When the chemical fingerprint of sewage – nitrogen and phosphate – rises, Chlamy blooms and turns a deeper, darker green. The faster this happens, the greater the risk to the river and everyone who depends on it.

Because water is transparent, we're working with Chlamy to make pollution visible – its colour is the data.

About this project

Algaerithm is a citizen science project by MA Biodesign students at Central Saint Martins, UAL, exploring what a citizen-led river monitoring network using microalgae could look like.

Anyone with a stretch of river and a reason to care – local folks, swimmers, people with children and pets – can become a River Guardian and host a floating pod. A spectral sensor and timelapse camera log the colour changes through time. The data is mapped, open, and free, so communities can finally see what's in their water and hold polluters to account.
